# World-Class Presentation Patterns

Reference patterns for suggesting improvements. Every suggestion must adapt these patterns to be Telegrafen-compliant.

---

## 1. The McKinsey Pyramid (Strategy Decks)

**Structure:** Situation → Complication → Resolution

**Key rules:**
- **Governing thought on every slide:** The title IS the takeaway. "Revenue grew 23% driven by M4M" — never "Revenue Overview"
- **Ghost deck test:** Read only the slide titles in sequence. They should tell the complete story.
- **Exhibit format:** Data/chart on the left (60%), insight callout on the right (40%)
- **So what? test:** Every slide must answer "Why does this matter?"
- **Pyramid logic:** Start with the answer, then provide supporting evidence (not the other way around)

**Telegrafen adaptation:**
- Governing thoughts → Bold DM Sans 800, #070452 on light slides
- Exhibits → White cards on statement blue (#1C16C5) background
- Key numbers → #00C8FF accent, oversized (2-3x body text)

## 2. The Apple Launch (Product Presentations)

**Structure:** Problem → Hero → Proof → CTA

**Key rules:**
- **Hero number:** One massive KPI per slide, readable from 10 meters
- **Progressive reveal:** Don't show everything at once. Build the argument.
- **Emotional contrast:** Problem slides are dark/tense → Solution slides are light/relieved
- **Minimal text:** If you can't say it in 10 words, it belongs in the appendix
- **The pause slide:** One slide that's just a powerful statement. No data, no bullets. Just the idea.

**Telegrafen adaptation:**
- Hero numbers → DM Sans 800, 4-5rem, #00C8FF on #1C16C5 background
- Problem slides → Statement blue (#1C16C5) with warning accent
- Solution slides → Light cyan (#EBFFFF) with success indicators
- Pause slides → Statement blue, centered text, max 8 words

## 3. The Bezos 6-Pager (A4 Documents)

**Structure:** Narrative prose, not bullets

**Key rules:**
- **Full sentences, not fragments.** Force rigorous thinking.
- **Read-first, discuss-after.** The document must be self-contained.
- **Data inline:** Numbers appear in context, not in appendix charts
- **FAQ section:** Preempt every question the reader might have
- **No PowerPoint:** The medium IS the message — prose signals depth

**Telegrafen adaptation:**
- Body text → DM Sans 400, 0.95rem, #070452, line-height 1.7
- Section headers → DM Sans 700, 1.5rem, 4px #00C8FF left border
- Key metrics inline → DM Sans 700, #00C8FF color
- A4 portrait, generous margins (25mm sides, 20mm top/bottom)

---

## 4. The TED Talk Structure (Keynotes)

**Structure:** Hook → 3 Acts → Memorable Close

**Key rules:**
- **Open with a story, not a logo slide.** The first 10 seconds determine engagement.
- **Rule of 3:** Three main points, three supporting facts, three slides per section max
- **The callback:** Reference the opening story in the closing
- **Visual metaphors:** Use ONE visual metaphor consistently (journey, engine, bridge)
- **End with a question or call to action**, not a "thank you" slide

**Telegrafen adaptation:**
- Hook slide → Full-bleed photo with #1C16C5 overlay and #00C8FF text
- Three acts → Three section dividers using Statement blue
- Close → Light cyan with centered bold statement

## 5. The Telenor Strategy Deck (This Workspace)

**Observed patterns from the reference PPTX template and example slides:**

### Slide Types (from template.pptx)

| Type | Background | Text | Use Case |
|------|-----------|------|----------|
| Light Content | #EBFFFF | #070452 | Default for detail, explanation, tables |
| Statement Blue | #1C16C5 | #FFFFFF | Key messages, punchlines, big numbers |
| Cards Layout | #1C16C5 | White cards with #070452 text | Comparing 2-4 options or pillars |
| Photo Divider | Full-bleed image | #1C16C5 overlay + #00C8FF text | Section breaks, emotional moments |
| Title Slide | #1C16C5 or #070452 | #FFFFFF + #00C8FF | Opening, closing |

### When to Use Statement Blue

Statement blue (#1C16C5) is NOT for decoration. Use it when:
- Presenting the single most important number on a slide
- Making a strategic declaration ("The future is license-based")
- Transitioning between major sections (divider)
- Presenting a summary/conclusion

Do NOT use statement blue for:
- Regular content slides (use light cyan #EBFFFF)
- Tables with lots of data (readability suffers)
- Every other slide mechanically (it loses meaning)

## Anti-Patterns (Score Killers)

These design patterns automatically cap scores:

| Anti-Pattern | Max Score | Fix |
|-------------|----------|-----|
| Wall of text (> 100 words on a slide) | Info Architecture: 5 | Split into 2 slides or move to appendix |
| No visual anchor (every element same size) | Visual Design: 5 | Make ONE thing 3x larger than everything else |
| Rainbow colors (5+ colors on one slide) | Brand Compliance: 4 | Max 3 Telegrafen colors per slide |
| Topic titles ("Revenue Overview") | Narrative Arc: 5 | Action titles ("Revenue grew 23%") |
| Identical slide layouts throughout | Visual Design: 7 | Vary between 3-4 Telegrafen patterns |
| Chart with no insight callout | Data Viz: 6 | Add bold annotation on the key data point |
| Orphan content (1 bullet point) | Info Architecture: 6 | Merge with adjacent content or expand |
| Gratuitous animation/effects | Executive Readiness: 6 | Remove all animation from strategy decks |
