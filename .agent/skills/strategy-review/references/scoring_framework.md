# Scoring Framework — Strategy Review

## Overview

Every strategy document is scored across **8 dimensions**. Each dimension has a defined weight reflecting its relative importance to overall strategy quality. Scores are 1-10, where:

- **1-3**: Fundamentally flawed or missing entirely
- **4-6**: Present but insufficient — material gaps that reduce effectiveness
- **7-8**: Solid — meets expectations with room for refinement
- **9-10**: Exceptional — best-in-class, would impress a board of directors

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Strategic Clarity & Alignment (Weight: 15%)

> Does the strategy explicitly link to Norgesstrategi ("Skape sammen, vinne kunden")? Does it articulate a clear Vision, supported by a maximum of 5 Strategic Pillars outlining the key problems to solve?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | Goals are vague. No clear link to Norgesstrategi. Missing a unifying vision or has too many unfocused pillars. |
| 4-6 | Mentions Norgesstrategi but the link is weak. Has pillars, but they describe *activities* rather than *problems to solve*. |
| 7-8 | Clear vision and 5 (or fewer) strategic pillars defining problems to solve. Good linkage to top-level Norgesstrategi. |
| 9-10 | Crystal-clear alignment. Every pillar is a precisely defined problem that, if solved, directly operationalizes the Norgesstrategi. A unifying, inspiring vision anchors the work. |

**Telco benchmark**: Most telcos score 4-5. They confuse activity with strategy. A good score requires ruthless prioritization.

---

### 2. Market Differentiation (Weight: 15%)

> Are we ahead of the curve or copying competitors? Is this a "me too" strategy?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | Pure copycat. Doing exactly what every other telco is doing with no unique angle. |
| 4-6 | Some differentiation, but easily replicable. No sustainable competitive moat. |
| 7-8 | Clear differentiators with some defensibility. Leverages unique assets (network, customer base, trust). |
| 9-10 | Bold, differentiated positioning. Creates new market categories or establishes first-mover advantage. Competitors would need years to replicate. |

**Telco benchmark**: Most B2B telco strategies score 3-4. True differentiation in telco requires leveraging network + data + trust assets in novel ways.

---

### 3. Commercial Viability (Weight: 15%)

> Is there a clear revenue model? Are TAM/SAM/SOM estimates realistic? Are financial targets credible?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | No revenue model. "We'll figure out monetization later." Fantasy market sizing. |
| 4-6 | Revenue model exists but assumptions are untested. Market sizing relies on third-party reports without validation. |
| 7-8 | Credible revenue model with validated assumptions. Realistic market sizing with bottom-up validation. Unit economics understood. |
| 9-10 | Detailed financial model with scenario analysis. Validated by customer conversations/pilots. Clear path from current revenue to target with milestones. |

**Telco benchmark**: Telcos often over-estimate B2B opportunity by 3-5x. Real score requires customer-validated demand.

---

### 4. Execution Feasibility (Weight: 15%)

> Can we actually do this? Do we have the capabilities, resources, and organizational readiness?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | Plan requires capabilities we don't have and can't realistically build. No resourcing plan. "We'll hire AI engineers" without a plan for how. |
| 4-6 | Partially feasible. Significant capability gaps acknowledged but mitigation is hand-wavy. Timeline is optimistic. |
| 7-8 | Feasible with clear capability building plan. Resource allocation defined. Realistic timeline with dependencies mapped. |
| 9-10 | Detailed execution plan with phased approach. Quick wins identified. Capability gaps addressed through build/buy/partner decisions. Contingency plans for key risks. |

**Telco benchmark**: This is where most telco strategies die. Great vision, terrible execution planning. Score 4-5 is typical.

---

### 5. Innovation & Future-Proofing (Weight: 10%)

> Does this strategy embrace emerging technology (AI, cloud, platform)? Will it still be relevant in 3-5 years?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | Technology is an afterthought. No mention of AI, automation, or platform thinking. Strategy could have been written in 2015. |
| 4-6 | Technology mentioned but bolt-on. "We'll use AI" without specifics. No platform strategy. |
| 7-8 | Technology is core to the strategy. Specific AI/automation use cases identified. Platform/ecosystem thinking present. |
| 9-10 | Technology-first strategy. AI-native operating model. Clear view on agentic AI, LLMs, cloud-native. Built for the 2027-2030 landscape, not today's. |

**Telco benchmark**: Most telcos score 4-5. They mention AI in every presentation but have no real plan for AI-native operations.

---

### 6. Customer & Value Proposition (Weight: 10%)

> Does this solve a real, painful customer problem? Is the value proposition compelling and validated?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | No evidence of customer input. Solution looking for a problem. Value proposition is "we offer great service." |
| 4-6 | Customer needs referenced but not validated. Value proposition is generic. Not clear why a customer would choose us over alternatives. |
| 7-8 | Value proposition grounded in validated customer pain points. Clear differentiation from alternatives. Segmentation present. |
| 9-10 | Deep customer insight driving the strategy. Validated through interviews/pilots. Clear "10x better" proposition for target segments. Customer-back design. |

**Telco benchmark**: Telco B2B strategies often score 3-4. They lead with technology, not customer problems.

---

### 7. Execution Architecture & Follow-up (Weight: 10%)

> Does the strategy translate into "Business Opportunities" as the primary prioritization mechanism for teams? Are there clear Customer Outcomes, Key Milestones, and Leading Indicators?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | No clear translation mechanism to team execution. Follow-up is ad-hoc or based purely on effort ("done/not done"). |
| 4-6 | Uses traditional project metrics instead of Business Opportunities. Missing strong leading indicators or customer-centric outcomes. |
| 7-8 | Prioritizes via Business Opportunities. Follow-up includes milestones and some leading indicators, but customer outcomes could be clearer. |
| 9-10 | Perfect execution architecture. Every initiative is framed as a Business Opportunity. Teams govern themselves via rigorously defined Customer Outcomes, Key Milestones, and predictive Leading Indicators. |

**Telco benchmark**: Score 3-4 typical. Telcos try to run transformative strategies through legacy org structures.

---

### 8. Risk & Dependency Management (Weight: 10%)

> Are risks identified, quantified, and owned? Are dependencies mapped?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | No risk section. "What could go wrong?" mentality. Dependencies not considered. |
| 4-6 | Risks listed at a high level but not quantified or owned. Dependencies acknowledged but not managed. |
| 7-8 | Key risks identified with probability/impact assessment. Mitigation strategies defined. Dependencies mapped with owners. |
| 9-10 | Comprehensive risk register with quantified impact. Pre-mortem analysis conducted. Dependency map with critical path identified. Escalation triggers defined. |

**Telco benchmark**: Score 5-6 typical. Telcos are decent at identifying risks but weak at quantifying and mitigating them.

---

## Weighted Score Calculation

```
Overall Score = Σ (Dimension Score × Dimension Weight)
```

| Dimension | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Strategic Clarity & Alignment | 15% |
| Market Differentiation | 15% |
| Commercial Viability | 15% |
| Execution Feasibility | 15% |
| Innovation & Future-Proofing | 10% |
| Customer & Value Proposition | 10% |
| Execution Architecture & Follow-up | 10% |
| Risk & Dependency Management | 10% |
| **Total** | **100%** |

### Overall Score Interpretation

| Range | Rating | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 8.5-10.0 | ⭐ Exceptional | Board-ready. World-class strategy. |
| 7.0-8.4 | ✅ Strong | Solid foundation. Refinement needed. |
| 5.0-6.9 | ⚠️ Needs Work | Material gaps. Not ready for execution. |
| 3.0-4.9 | ❌ Weak | Fundamental rethinking required. |
| 1.0-2.9 | 🚨 Critical | Start over. This is not a strategy. |

---

## Dimension Applicability

Not all dimensions apply equally to all document types. Use this guide:

| Document Type | Key Dimensions (double weight) | Deprioritized Dimensions |
|---------------|-------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Executive Summary | Strategic Clarity & Alignment, Market Differentiation | Risk Management |
| Transformation Plan | Execution Feasibility, Execution Architecture & Follow-up | Market Differentiation |
| Business Case | Commercial Viability, Customer/VP | Innovation |
| Roadmap | Execution Feasibility, Risk Management | Commercial Viability |
| Go-to-Market Plan | Customer/VP, Market Differentiation | Execution Architecture & Follow-up |

When a dimension is deprioritized, its weight shifts to the key dimensions proportionally. Document this in the review output.
