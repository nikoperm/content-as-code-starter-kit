# Execution Framework

## Business Opportunity (BO) Structure

Every strategic initiative in this workspace is structured as a **Business Opportunity (BO)**. A BO is the atomic unit of strategic execution — it connects a customer need to a measurable business outcome.

### Required Fields

| Field | Description | Example |
|:------|:-----------|:--------|
| **Customer Outcome** | What the customer gets | "Reduced security incidents by 50%" |
| **Value Proposition** | Why this matters to the organization | "Recurring revenue from managed security" |
| **Financial Target** | Revenue or margin goal (3-year) | "XX MNOK by 2029" |
| **Milestones** | Key deliverables with dates | "MVP launch Q3 2026" |
| **Leading Indicators** | Early signals of progress | "Pipeline value, pilot conversions" |
| **Owner** | Person or team responsible | "Security Team" |

## Governance Model

### Strategic Areas

Initiatives are grouped into strategic areas, each with autonomous cross-functional teams:

1. **Core Connectivity** — Protect and optimize the base business
2. **Secure by Design** — Security as a service layer
3. **Customer Solutions** — Vertical-specific B2B solutions
4. **Efficient Admin** — Self-service and operational automation
5. **Intelligent Communication** — Next-gen unified communications

### Cadence

| Activity | Frequency | Purpose |
|:---------|:----------|:--------|
| Team standup | Weekly | Progress on active BOs |
| Area review | Monthly | Cross-BO alignment and resource allocation |
| Portfolio review | Quarterly | Strategic prioritization and rebalancing |

## Success Measurement

### Three Levels

1. **Customer Outcomes** — Did we deliver what we promised?
2. **Key Milestones** — Are we hitting our delivery targets?
3. **Leading Indicators** — Do early signals predict success?

### Scoring

Each BO is evaluated on:
- **Progress**: On track / At risk / Behind
- **Confidence**: High / Medium / Low
- **Financial impact**: Tracking to target / Below / Above
